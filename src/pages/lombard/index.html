Our Work

benefits that truly stand out

Facility managers (FM's) are our customers just as much as the company they work for. FM's are often tasked with completing hundreds of projects per year in multiple states. As one can imagine this is a very daunting task, and one that requires a company that possesses not only the general contracting abilities to successfully complete a large-scale project, but also effectively skilled to manage the logistics and procurement elements to our FM's workload.

The one benefits that truly stands out is the tremendous reduction in time our FM's spend on the minutia. Our record of excellence has built a sizeable positive impact on how our FM's projects are budgeted, properly supervised, documented through photographs, and completed on time and within budget; while our projects rarely involve change orders.

In addition, our corporate clients have experienced: (1) reduced budgets (2) increased control of project flow (3) higher annual and/or quarterly project completion rates and more.

Our Services

in-house general contracting throughout the east coast

Lombard currently provides in-house general contracting services throughout the east coast ranging from Upstate New York to Miami, Florida. In locales where Lombard does not have a team/crew in place, the services required for the project are procured by Lombard procurement professionals. Our procurement professionals are certified in procurement (FAC-C: Federal Acquisition Certification - Contracting) from the Department of Homeland Security.

About Us

belief statement

Our secret to success has been to do things the Lombard Way!

Lombard defines professionalism and integrity in commercial contracting & facility management simply as: (1) being available for our clients to address needs without prior notice; (2) providing carefully crafted estimates; (3) completing work to a high degree of quality; (4) documenting our work by providing before, during and after pictures for projects; (5) remaining engaged with our clients as a fully integrated partner throughout the project lifecycle by returning to a project and/or working through issues that may arise; (6) and last, but certainly not least, the little important things such as: answering the telephone and responding to email correspondences in a timely fashion.
